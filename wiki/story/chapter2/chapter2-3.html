2.3 [[Location: Applewood Elementary / Airi’s Elementary School]
[Scene Description]:

Airi travels to the past with Mari, visiting her elementary school, where she notices a kid version of herself, who’s in second grade in this time period. They arrive at the school entrance, where there is a building in front of them, and various playground types of equipment - a sandpit with swing, monkey bars, bouncy horses on the right side. There are benches and paved roads on the left side. Kid Airi is sitting against the wall of the building, sipping her juices while reading a book. There are 3 girls playing jump rope, one of them being a kid version of Dara from chapter 1.

Inside the building is first a hallway, with 2 classrooms on one side, and an stadium and bathroom on the other. One of the classrooms will be just for show, while the other one will have a cutscene. The one with the cutscene has two boys playing a video game on two Gameboys (jokingly dubbed Gamegirls in the game), and a girl reading a manga. Both classrooms are typical classroom settings, with a dark green chalkboard on one side with the teacher’s desk, and tall windows that let the students see the outside. The bathroom won’t be important, but there will be a cutscene in the stadium. There is a group of boys and girls playing basketball on one side of the court in the stadium and a ballrack near by.

At the end of the hallway are the teacher offices and the stair to the second floor. The teacher offices are more Japanese-like than American, where teachers have their own cubicles instead. However, due to the scope of the game the player won’t be able to explore the teacher’s office, and when the player is in there it will entirely be a cutscene. Thus the only important description will be one of Airi’s teacher cubicles, where there will be Airi’s parents, Takeda and Yumiko, and kid Airi on one side of the table, and her teacher on the other. If the player tries to enter the teacher office, Airi will prevents them, saying "I don't think Mari is in there, let's check other place for now"

On the 2nd floor, there will also be another staircase to the 3rd floor. There will be a music classroom in the same location as the teacher offices on the first floor (but on the second floor), two more classrooms with no cutscenes on one side, and a row of lockers, another no cutscenes classroom (5 classrooms in total, one for each grade) and another set of bathrooms on the other. The music classroom will be a typical classroom except the teacher’s desk will be moved more toward the center, leaving the corner for a grand piano.

The 3rd floor is very small. There is a small room on the teacher’s office side which is just the storage room that isn’t relevant to the story. There will be an exit to the rooftop of the building on the other, and a cutscene will happen there. The roof top is pretty much empty, and during the cutscene, kid version of Airi will be sitting down against the wall next to the entrance

Obviously, all these details are described because they are important to the scenes. There will be various unnamed NPC/kids and teachers the player can interact with but won’t have any significance. At the beginning of this part, a door will appear out of thin air, and Airi and Mari will walk out of it. This door is a rectangle shape, with the outline kind of like laser cutting through metal showing up first before the portal is open (think of the circle outline in Dr. Strange showing up before the portal is open)


Airi and Mari go to Applewood Elementary school to see if they can find any clues about Mari. Mari remembers that she attended elementary school. Despite being able to find young Airi at the school, they couldn’t find young Mari. Airi and Mari enjoy their time, with Airi having nostalgia about playing BapaMon on her Gamegirl and reading Detective O’Brien. Toward the end, Mari accidentally enters the teacher’s office, where she and Airi listen to Airi’s parents Takeda and Yumiko arguing with Airi’s teacher, asking for Airi to be put in advance classes to prepare her for college despite the fact that she's only in 2nd grade. Airi opened up to Mari a little bit about her family background, how her parents used to push her to do well, but then stopped once her brother was born, due to Asian cultural preference for boys, and instead pushed her brother instead. They return to the Shooting Star Express.

[Scene Description]: The train station will be the same as before since this is a continuation of the last scene from chapter 1. Airi first talks to the fiery skeleton on the train platform, before going back inside the train. It is a running joke that the fiery skeleton speak with an Australian accent and Airi mispronounce his name
The fiery skeleton introduces himself to be Astraeus. He explains to Airi and Mari that he’s the God of the Shooting Star. Part of Astraeus’s duty as a god is to guide the spirit of the dead to the afterlife. He reveals to Mari that she suicided. Due to some unknown reason, Astraeus offers to give Mari a second chance at life, provided that she wants to live after she recovers her memories. Since Airi wished for a more interesting life, Astraeus brought Airi here to give her “the adventure of a lifetime”. He asks Airi to help Mari by taking Mari through Airi’s childhood, revealing that Mari also grew up in Airi’s hometown. Airi agrees to help and everyone enters the Shooting Star Express.
